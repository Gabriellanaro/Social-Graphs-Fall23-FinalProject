Khichdi is a mild-tasting Gujarati rice and lentil dish.

There are many regional variations to this basic recipe.

===Ingredients===
| 1 cup of white basmati rice, rinsed in a sieve under cold running water
| 1 cup of yellow lentils, picked clear of stones, washed and soaked in water then drained
| 4 cups of water
| ½ teaspoon cumin seeds
| ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
| 1 teaspoon of vegetable oil
| sea salt to taste
===Method===


| Heat the oil in a large heavy pot
| When oil is hot, add cumin seeds and let them fry and pop for a second or two
| Immediately add  the rice ,lentils and turmeric powder. Stir well to mix
| Add the water and salt and bring to the boil.
| Reduce the heat and simmer with the lid ajar
| Lower the heat, partially cover the pot and cook until the rice and lentils are cooked - about 35 minutes
===Serving suggestions===

Serve hot with yogurt
