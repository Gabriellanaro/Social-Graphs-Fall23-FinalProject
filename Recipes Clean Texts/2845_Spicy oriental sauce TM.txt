===Description===

This is a translation of a recipe in '''Salsas y Ali\u00f1os con Thermomix (Susaeta Ediciones)'''

===Ingredients===TM
| 100g sweet red peppers
| 2 red guindilla chillies
| 6 Cloves garlic, peeled
| 2 small spring onions
| 2 tablespoons tomato concentrate
| 70g virgin olive oil
| 1 tablespoon fresh coriander
| 1 teaspoon ground cumin
| 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
| 1 teaspoon sugar
| 2 tablespoons soya sauce
| Salt
===Method===


| Wash the guindillas and remove the stalks.
| Cut them into several pieces and reserve,
| Place the peeled and chopped spring onions and the garlic into the bowl and blitz '''10 seconds / Speed 5'''.
| Add the oil and program '''7 minutes / Temp. Varoma / Speed 1'''.
| Next, add the tomato concentrate, the peppers and the guindillas '''5 minutes / Temp. Varoma / Speed 1'''.
| Afterwards, add the soya sauce, vinegar, cumin and the sugar and program '''10 minutes / 100\u00b0 / Speed 2'''.
| Once finished, add the coriander and a pinch of salt and blend '''10 seconds / Speed 5'''.
| Allow to cool and transfer into airtight jars.
===Serving suggestions===
This sauce can be served cold or hot.  It is an ideal accompaniment to oriental dishes and all types of roast meats.  
===Chef's notes===
It is very spicy and therefore it is recommended that it be served in moderation.  It is advisable to wear gloves when handling the guindillas and avoid contact with the eyes.
