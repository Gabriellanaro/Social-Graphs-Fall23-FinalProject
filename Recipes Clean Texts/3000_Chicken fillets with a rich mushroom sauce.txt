===Description===

Cheesey [https://www.cookipedia.co.uk/cgi-bin/cc.pl?cftk=2#jump chicken pieces] in a rich mushroom sauce.  This recipe comes to Cookipedia courtesy of D\u017eiugas cheeses.
===Ingredients===

====for the cheesey chicken pieces====
===Ingredients===NB
| 2 Chicken fillets
| 1 free range egg
| 40 g\tof D\u017eiugas cheese
| sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
| 1 tablespoon plain flour
| 2 tablespoons\tof vegetable oil (for frying)
| 50 g\tof butter
====for the creamy mushroom sauce====
===Ingredients===NB
| 30 g\tfinely chopped onion
| 1 clove of garlic, peeled and crushed
| 60 g\tof mushrooms, finely chopped
| 100 g Cr\u00e8me fra\u00eeche
| 50 ml water

===Method===


| Ensure the chicken pieces are about the same size
| Cover them with cling film and bash them with a rolling pin to flatten so they cook quickly
| Beat the egg and mix in a tablespoon of the double cream, the D\u017eiugas cheese and season with salt and black pepper
| Add the oil and butter to a large frying pan and heat to a medium heat
| Pop the chicken pieces into a Lock & Lock box together with a tablespoon of plain flour, close the lid and shake to coat evenly
| Dip the floured chicken pieces in the egg and cream mix Flour the pieces, then dip them into egg and cheese mixture
| Fry the chicken pieces for about 10 minutes, turning once.
| Check the chicken is properly cooked with a meat thermometer; 74 \u00b0C (165 \u00b0F)
| Drain on kitchen paper
====mushroom sauce====

| Fry onions and mushrooms, add garlic, cr\u00e8me fra\u00eeche and water.
| Bring to a gentle simmer and cook for a couple of minutes
| Season to taste & serve
===Variations===
If you cannot obtain D\u017eiugas cheese, a good quality Parmesan will work equally well.
