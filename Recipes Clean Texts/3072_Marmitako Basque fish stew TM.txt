===Description===
A Thermomix version of this famous dish from the Basque Country where it is known as Marmita-kua

'''For the sofrito'''
===Ingredients===
| 50g extra virgin olive oil
| 100g green peppers
| 150g onions
| 100g tinned tomatoes, crushed
'''For the marmitako'''
===Ingredients===NB
| 2 teaspoons choricero pepper paste or 1 tablespoon paprika
| 800g potatoes, but into large cubes
| 400g fish stock or water
| 300g bonito or fresh tuna, without skin or bones, cut into fingers
| 2 tablespoons chopped parsley
===Method===


| Tip all of the sofrito ingredients into the bowl and chop '''4 seconds / Speed 4'''.
| Program '''7 minutes / Temp. Varoma / Speed 1'''.
| Add the choricero pepper paste or paprika and mix for a '''few seconds / Speed 3'''.
| Next, place the '''Butterly''' on the blades add the potatoes  and cook '''2 minutes / 100\u00b0 / Reverse Blade / Speed Spoon'''.
| Add the fish stock or water and the salt '''20 minutes / Temp. Varoma / Reverse Blade / Speed Spoon'''.
| Add the bonito or tuna '''3 minutes / Temp. Varoma / Reverse Blade / Speed Spoon'''.
| When the machine stops, incorporate the chopped parsley.
| Adjust seasoning and leave for a few minutes without removing the bowl from the machine.
===Serving suggestions===
Serve with country bread. 
===Recipe source===
* This is a translation of a recipe in '''''Thermomix TM31 - Imprescindible para tu cocina''''' which is the book supplied with Spanish TMXs
