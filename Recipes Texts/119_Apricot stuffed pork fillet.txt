in a creamy [[Mushroom|mushroom]] [[Sauce|sauce]].\n{{RecipeIngredients\n\n| 1 whole [[Pork fillet|pork fillet]], about 500g (1 lb)\n| 50 g (2 oz) [[Dried apricots|dried apricots]] (chopped), soaked [[Freshly ground black pepper|freshly ground black pepper]].\n\n}}\n\n===Method===\n\n{{RecipeMethod\n\n| [[How to butterfly a chicken breast|Butterfly]] the pork fillet lengthways. ''Cut it open like a book''.\n| Flatten a little more using a [[Rolling pin|rolling pin]] or a [[Meat mallet|meat mallet]].\n| Season the inside with a little [[Salt|salt]] and [[Pepper|pepper]].\n| Arrange the chopped [[Apricots|apricots]] and chopped [[Sage leaves|sage leaves]] along one side.\n| Sprinkle with the chopped [[Parsley|parsley]].\n| Close the ''book'' and secure with cocktail sticks or tie with fine string.\n| Heat the tablespoon of [[Olive oil|olive oil]] in a large [[Frying pan|frying pan]] over a medium heat. Sprinkle with a little [[Black pepper|black pepper]] and [[Brown|brown]] the [[Pork fillet|pork fillet]], turning regularly so it colours evenly.\n| Remove and keep warm.\n| Add the chopped [[Onions|onions]] and [[garlic]] and saut\u00e9 until softened but not coloured.\n| Add the [[Stock|stock]], [[Lime|lime]] or [[Lemon juice|lemon juice]] to the [[Onions|onions]], add the [[Meat|meat]] and cover and [[Simmer|simmer]] for 20 minutes.\n| Add the sliced [[Mushrooms|mushrooms]] and the [[Sherry|sherry]], stir well, cover and [[Simmer|simmer]] for another 15 minutes.\n| Remove the [[Pork|pork]] and discard the string or cocktail sticks, set to one side and keep warm.\n| Stir in the [[Soured cream|soured cream]] and warm through.  Don't allow it to [[Boil|boil]] or it will separate.\n| Slice the [[Pork fillet|pork fillet]] into medallions and arrange on serving plates.\n| Spoon the [[Mushrooms|mushrooms]] and [[Sauce|sauce]] over the [[Pork|pork]].\n}}\n<gallery widths=300px heights=300px perrow=5>\nImage:Apricot_stuffed_pork_fillet_sewn_up.jpg|I found it more secure to tie with [[kitchen string]], using [[cocktail sticks]] to hold it in place while I tied the fillet.\nImage:Apricot_stuffed_pork_fillet_butterflied_and_stuffed.jpg|[[Butterflied]] and stuffing placed on one side\n</gallery>\n\n===Serving suggestions===\nServe on a bed of [[Plain rice|plain rice]] garnished with a few fresh [[Sage leaves|sage leaves]].\n===Variations===\nServe with [[pasta]], [[plain boiled rice]] or [[Boiled|boiled]] [[New Potatoes|new potatoes]].\n====The best knot to tie kitchen twine single-handedly====\nAfter much experimenting, I have discovered the best knot to tie kitchen twine on your own is the ''[[Surgeons' knot]]''.\n===Chef's notes===\nFollow the [[RSPCA Think Pig Checklist]] to make sure that your [[Pork|pork]] has come from pigs that have been reared to higher welfare standards.\n\n* http://bit.ly/rspcathinkpig\n{{RecipeLine}}\n[[Category:Recipes]]\n[[Category:British recipes]]\n[[Category:Main courses]]\n[[Category:Fruit recipes]]\n[[Category:Pork recipes]]\n[[Category:Stewed, braised or pot roasted]]\n[[Category:Unusual recipes]]\n\n<!-- footer hashtags --><code 'hashtagrev:032020'>#porkfillet #sherry #pork #apricotstuffedporkfillet #sageleaves #garlic #parsley #sauce #mushrooms #apricots #lemonjuice </code><!-- /footer_hashtags -->"
