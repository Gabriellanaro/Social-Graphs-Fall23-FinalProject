It is essentially a stew prepared with beef [[Braised|braised]] in a full bodied [[Red wine|red wine]] and [[Beef stock|beef stock]], generally flavoured with [[Garlic|garlic]], [[Onions|onions]], [[Carrots|carrots]], and a [[Bouquet garni|bouquet garni]], and garnished with pearl [[Onions|onions]] and [[Mushrooms|mushrooms]].\n\nA WMF Perfect Plus 6.5 litre [[pressure cooker]] has recently joined my stash of [[kitchen tools|kitchen toys]] and I've been experimenting with the best way to cook beef bourguignon in a pressure cooker.  I was after a fully-flavoured dish rather than a fast one; this was the result.\n\nIf you want the authentic, non-pressure cooker version, [[Beef bourguignon recipe|you can find it here]].\n\n'''the marinade'''\n{{RecipeIngredients\n| 300 ml full bodied [[red wine]]\n}}\n\n'''[[Bouquet garni]]'''\n{{RecipeIngredientsNB\n| 3 [[Bay leaf|bay leaves]]\n| 1 huge stem of [[cutting celery]], 'flowers and all' - A stalk of [[celery]], ideally with leaves would be fine\n| 1 sprig of [[Parsley|parsley]]\n| 1 small sprig of [[Thyme|thyme]]\n}}\n'''the stew''' \n{{RecipeIngredientsNB\n| 2 tablespoons [[Olive oil]]\n| 3 large [[banana shallots]], peeled and cut in half\n| 4 cloves of [[garlic]], peeled and crushed\n| 2 [[Portobello mushrooms]], (large field mushrooms), roughly sliced\n| 2 [[carrots]], peeled and chopped\n| 250 g [[Stewing]] [[Steak|steak]], trimmed of excess [[Fat|fat]] and cut into bite-sized cubes\n| 10 [[cherry tomatoes]] - any type will do, they happened to be spare\n| 2 tablespoons of [[tomato puree]]\n| Half an [[oxo cube]], crumbled\n| [[Sea salt]] and freshly ground [[black pepper]]\n}}\n'''to thicken'''\n{{RecipeIngredientsNB\n| 1 tablespoon of [[cornflour]]\n}}\n'''to serve'''\n{{RecipeIngredientsNB\n| 240g of dried [[egg noodles]], cooked as directed on the packet\n}}\n===Method===\n\n{{RecipeMethod\n| If you have the time beforehand, add the meat, half bottle of red wine, the cubed steak and the [[bouquet garni]] (tied with [[kitchen string]]) to a [[Lock and Lock]]-style box, cover, shake and refrigerate for a few hours or more.\n| Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large pan or wok.  You can use the pressure cooker for this but I've found it sometimes stick during the cooking so do any 'beforehand' saut\u00e9ing in my trusty [[wok]]. \n| Add a good grind of [[black pepper]], the [[shallots]] and the [[garlic]] and gently [[saut\u00e9]] 5 minutes, stirring now and then\n| Add the mushrooms and carrots and fry for another 5 minutes.\n| Tip the onions, garlic, mushrooms and carrots into the pressure cooker.\n| Add the meat and red wine marinade, reserving the [[bouquet garni]].\n| Crumble half an [[oxo cube]] into the cooker together with the [[tomatoes]] and [[tomato puree]] and stir well.\n| Sit the [[bouquet garni]] in the perforated trivet and sit this atop of the stew.\n| Cover and bring the pressure cooker to [[:Category:Pressure_cooker_recipes#Internal_temperature_related_to_pressure|high pressure]]\n| Cook for 1 hour\n| [[De-pressurising a pressure cooker|De-pressurise naturally]], during this time boil the noodles as directed\n| If the stew needs thickening, mix to a paste;
