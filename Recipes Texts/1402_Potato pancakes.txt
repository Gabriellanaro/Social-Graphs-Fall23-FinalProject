[[Potato]] [[Pancake|pancake]]s may be topped with a variety of [[Condiments|condiments]], ranging from the [[Savory|savoury]] (such as [[Sour cream|sour cream]]) to the sweet (such as applesauce or [[Sugar|sugar]]), or they may be served ungarnished. Potato pancakes are sometimes made from [[Mashed potatoes|mashed potatoes]] to produce pancake-shaped croquettes.\n\n[[Potato pancakes]] are commonly associated with traditional Czech, Ukrainian, Yiddish, [[German]], Hungarian and Polish cuisine, although other cuisines (including those of India and Korea) also have similar [[Potato|potato]] [[Pancakes|pancakes]]. It is also the national dish of Belarus. In Ukrainian, Belarusian, and Russian cuisines potato pancakes are commonly known as deruny (Ukrainian: \u0434\u0435\u0440\u0443\u043d\u0438) or draniki (Russian: \u0434\u0440\u0430\u043d\u0438\u043a\u0438, Belarusian: \u0414\u0440\u0430\u043d\u0456\u043a\u0456). In Bavaria, Germany, potato pancakes are also very much liked under the name Reiberdatschi or [[Kartoffelpuffer]], and they are eaten either salty as a [[Side dish|side dish]] or sweet with [[Apple sauce|apple sauce]] and [[Cinnamon|cinnamon]].\n\nA form of [[Potato pancake|potato pancake]] known as '[[Boxty]]' is a popular traditional dish in most of Ireland. It is made in a similar way but using more [[Starch|starch]].\n\nThe Swedish version of unbound [[Potato pancakes|potato pancakes]] are called r\u00e5rakor. When prepared with a [[Batter|batter]] of [[Wheat flour|wheat flour]], [[Milk|milk]], [[Egg|egg]], and [[Shredded|shredded]] [[Potatoes|potatoes]] and [[Fried|fried]] like thin [[Pancakes|pancakes]], they are called raggmunk, which literally translates as \"hairy [[Doughnuts|doughnuts]]\" (the [[Grated|grated]] potatoes make them look hairy). Both kinds are enjoyed with fried [[Bacon|bacon]] and [[Lingonberry|lingonberry]] [[Jam|jam]].\n\n[[Tortitas de papa (Potato pancakes)]], is a [[Mexican]] version of the potato
