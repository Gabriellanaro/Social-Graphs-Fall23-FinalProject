Taro balls in coconut cream is a famous ancient [[Thai]] [[Dessert|dessert]]. \n\nThe main ingredients of Bau Loy are [[Flour|flour]], [[Sugar|sugar]], and [[Coconut milk|coconut milk]]. The definition of [[Bua loy]] comes from the flour, which is moulded in to the pot, put in the [[Boiling|boiling]] water and bring back to the [[Boil|boil]] until it floats Today, Bau loy has a little change from the original by add more ingredients , for example [[Taro|taro]] ,Khai-whan ,or add the colour of flour by using the colour of flowers and food likes, green (Bai-toey), violet ([[Butterfly|butterfly]] [[Pea|pea]]),and black ([[Roasted|roasted]] [[Coconut|coconut]] ),or yellow  ([[Pumpkin|pumpkin]]).\n\n{{RecipeIngredients\n\n| 1 cup of thick [[Coconut cream|coconut cream]] obtained by [[Grating|grating]] [[Coconut|coconut]] [[Meat|meat]]\n| 2 cups of thin [[Coconut cream|coconut cream]]\n| 3 cups of [[Sugar|sugar]] (or add more sugar,if you like it very sweet)\n| 4 teaspoons of [[Salt|salt]] [[Flour]] in [[Bua loy|bua loy]]\n| 1 cup of [[Glutinous rice|glutinous rice]] [[Flour|flour]]\n| Colours from food (if you like the colourful)\n}}\n\n===Method===\n{{RecipeMethod\n\n| Mix the [[Flour|flour]] with water and [[Knead|knead]] it into a [[Dough|dough]]. Make sure it ready to [[Mould|mould]] it into the small balls , check it by touch the dough (the dough will be sticky).\n| Put the balls of [[Dough|dough]] into [[Boiling|boiling]] water and wait until the balls float on the surface of the water, spoon them up and put them in cold water to make them be stable.\n| make creamy [[Coconut milk|coconut milk]] by Mixing two cups of thin [[Coconut cream|coconut cream]] with [[Sugar|sugar]] and [[Salt|salt]].\n| place the pot of the mixed [[Coconut milk|coconut milk]] on a moderate heat, When it boils, put the balls in it.\n| After that, bring the mixture to the [[Boil|boil]] again, pour the thick [[Coconut cream|coconut cream]] into it and remove it from heat immediately without stirring it.\n| Bau Loy is ready to serve.\n}}\n==See also==\n\n* Pandarin Janesida.
