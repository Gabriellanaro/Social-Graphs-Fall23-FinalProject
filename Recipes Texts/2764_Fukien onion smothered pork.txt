It's really worth making.\n\n{{RecipeIngredients\n| 2.5 tablespoons of [[vegetable oil]]\n| 1kg [[belly of pork]] or spare rib chops\n| 1 teaspoon sea [[salt]]\n| 1 tablespoon [[light soy sauce]]\n| 2.5 tablespoons [[hoisin sauce]] \n| 2 teaspoons [[sugar]]\n| 3 [[onions]], peeled and sliced\n| 4 [[spring onions]] (scallions), [[cut on the diagonal]] into 2 \" pieces\n| 4 slices fresh [[root ginger]], finely chopped \n| 290 ml (&#189; pint) stock ([[chicken stock]] is fine)\n| 3 tablespoons [[rice wine]] or [[dry sherry]]\n\n}}\n\n===[[Mise en place]]===\n\n* Preheat the oven to 200\u00b0C (400\u00b0F - Gas Mk 6 - Moderately hot)\n\n===Method===\n\n{{RecipeMethod\n| Cut the belly into 3\" pieces.\n| Sprinkle with [[salt]] and [[pepper]] and rub with 1 tablespoon of [[oil]]\n| Heat the remaining [[oil]] in a wok\n| When hot add the [[ginger]] and the sliced [[onions]] and [[stir fry]] for 4 minutes or until slightly coloured, stirring often.\n| Add all the pork pieces and [[stir fry]] until the surface colour changes, about 4 minutes.\n| Add the [[soy sauce]], [[hoisin sauce]] and the [[sugar]], mix well and mix well.\n| Add the stock and bring to the boil\n| Transfer to a medium sized casserole and place in the oven for 1 hour\n| After 1 hour, reduce the heat to 150 C and leave for a further 1.5 hours\n| Remove from oven, add chopped [[spring onions]] and [[sherry]] and serve\n}}\n===Serving suggestions===\n\nServe with [[Boiled rice|plain boiled rice]]\n===Chef's notes===\n\nI used 1 large [[pork tenderloin fillet]] for 2 greedy people as I prefer it to the more fatty rib cut.
