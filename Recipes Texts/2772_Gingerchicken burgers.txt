Don't over-do it or you'll end up with baby-food.\n| Add the remaining ingredients and give it a quick pulse.\n| Tip out onto a cutting board and make the final mix with your hands.\n| Shape into 8 [[Burgers|burgers]].\n| Place on an oiled [[Barbecue|barbecue]] [[Grill|grill]], hotplate, [[Griddle|griddle]], or even a [[Frying pan|frying pan]].\n| Cook for about 8 minutes, turning every two minutes.\n| Check they are properly cooked.\n| Rest for 4 minutes.\n}}\n===Serving suggestions===\nServe with [[Satay sauce|satay sauce]].\n===Chef's notes===\nUse [[Chili sauce|chili sauce]] if you don't have fresh [[Chillies|chillies]].\n\nFreeze the uncooked burgers, separated with greaseproof paper for easy thawing.\n\nWith in the proportions above, you should not need a beaten egg to hold the burgers together, however, if you increase the quantity of breadcrumbs over 40g , then add a beaten egg at step one.\n==See also==\n* {{See also - Jointing a chicken}}\n{{Template:PeelingGinger}}\n{{RecipeLine}}\n[[Category:Recipes|Chicken]]\n[[Category:Barbecue recipes|Chicken]]\n[[Category:Oriental cuisine|Chicken]]\n[[Category:Main courses|Chicken]]\n[[Category:Meat recipes|Chicken]]\n[[Category:Poultry recipes|Chicken]]\n[[Category:Snacks and light bites|Chicken]]\n[[Category:Grilled, griddled or barbecued]]\n\n<!-- footer hashtags --><code 'hashtagrev:032020'>#gingerchickenburgers #chicken #sataysauce #barbecue #burgers #griddled #ginger #grill #foodprocessor #barbecued #darksoysauce </code><!-- /footer_hashtags -->"
