In India, it is called ribbon fish (not to be confused with ribbon fish of the ''Trachipteridae'' family).  It can also be called cutlass fish (amongst others) and in Portugal is called espada (meaning sword and not to be confused with [[swordfish]]).  Similarly in France, it is known as scabbard sword fish.  In the UK, it is sold as ribbon fish, because it comes from India.  It is a very inexpensive fish and in the UK can be obtain frozen from '''[http://www.frozenfishdirect.co.uk/?gclid=CLvz_Jv3urYCFajLtAodtxwA4g Frozen Fish Direct]'''.\n\n{{RecipeIngredients\n\n| 1kg ribbon [[Fish|fish]] (cutlass [[Fish|fish]]) fillets, cut into 6cm pieces\n| 100ml [[White wine vinegar|white wine vinegar]]\n| [[Garlic]] to taste, crushed\n| 100ml [[Dry white wine|dry white wine]]\n| 1 [[Bay leaf|bay leaf]]\n| 1 whole [[Clove|clove]]\n| 1 sprig [[Thyme|thyme]]\n| 1 sprig [[Parsley|parsley]]\n| [[Flour]] for dredging\n| [[Olive oil]] for [[Frying|frying]]\n\n}}\n\n===[[Mise en place]]===\n\n* Place the [[Fish|fish]] in a bowl with all of the ingredients (except the [[Flour|flour]]) and [[Marinate|marinate]] for up to 24 hours.\n===Method===\n\n{{RecipeMethod\n\n| Drain the [[Fish|fish]], reserving the [[Marinade|marinade]]'\n| Lightly dredge with the [[flour]] and [[Fry|fry]] in hot [[Oil|oil]] on both sides until cooked.\n| [[Boil]] the [[Marinade|marinade]] separately at the same time until reduced by half.\n| Place the [[Fish|fish]] in a serving dish and pour over the marinade.\n}}\n===Serving suggestions===\nThis is often served with '[[Milho Frito|milho frito]]' which is similar to fried [[Polenta|polenta]] or [[Boiled potatoes|boiled potatoes]] and [[Onions|onions]].\n===Recipe source===\n* Adapted from a recipe on '''[http://www.saborosas.com/receita/2007/Peixes/Mar/Espada%20vinho%20e%20alhos.aspx Saborosas]'''\n{{RecipeLine}}\n[[Category:Recipes|Fish wine garlic]]\n[[Category:Portuguese recipes|Fish wine garlic]]\n[[Category:Main courses|Fish wine garlic]]\n[[Category:Fish recipes|Fish wine garlic]]\n[[Category:Sea fish recipes|Fish wine garlic]]\n[[Category:Marinaded|Fish wine garlic]]\n[[Category:Pan fried|Fish wine garlic]]\n\n<!-- footer hashtags --><code 'hashtagrev:032020'>#fish #flour #marinade #fry #boil #panfriedfishwinegarlic #miseenplace #thyme #bayleaf #garlic #onions </code><!-- /footer_hashtags -->"
