Escalivada means \"cooked in hot ashes\" which is the authentic way to roast the [[Vegetables|vegetables]].  However, not everyone has hot ashes to hand, so the following method uses a [[Grill|grill]].\n\n{{RecipeIngredients\n| 125 ml [[Olive oil|olive oil]] or more\n| 4 [[Green peppers|sweet peppers]]\n| 4 [[Aubergines|aubergines]]\n| [[Salt]]\n| [[Parsley]], finely chopped\n}}\n===Method===\n\n\n'' see [[Roasting red peppers|Roasting peppers]]''\n{{RecipeMethod\n| [[Grill]] the [[Peppers|peppers]] and [[Aubergines|aubergines]] until the skins are blackened.  Peel.\n| Remove the [[Seeds|seeds]] and cut into strips. Place strips in a bowl and sprinkle with [[Salt|salt]] and allow to cool. \n| Dress the [[Vegetables|vegetables]] with [[Olive oil|olive oil]] and sprinkle with chopped [[Parsley|parsley]].\n}}\n===Serving suggestions===\nTraditionally served with [[Grilled|grilled]] [[Meat|meat]], it also makes an excellent tapa or first course.\n===Variations===\nYou can use any additional [[Grilled|grilled]] or [[Roasted|roasted]] [[Vegetables|vegetables]] you like, especially [[Tomatoes|tomatoes]] and [[Onions|onions]].\n\nYou may also wish to add Crushed [[Garlic|garlic]] to the [[Oil|oil]].\n===Chef's notes===\nThe [[Pepper|pepper]] and [[Aubergines|aubergines]] taste much better if [[Grilled|grilled]] on a barbecue. As fresh peas are only available for a short period of the year, you will probably use frozen ones for this recipe.  Note that the weight given above is for fresh peas, so you should [[defrost]] frozen peas, drain and pat dry before weighing.
