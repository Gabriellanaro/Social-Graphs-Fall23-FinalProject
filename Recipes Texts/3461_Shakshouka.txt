It is very similar to the Turkish dish Menemen and to the Latin American breakfast dish [[Huevos Rancheros|huevos rancheros]].\n\nIn Israeli cuisine, the dish is made of [[Eggs|eggs]], [[Tomatoes|tomatoes]], and [[Onions|onions]] or [[Garlic|garlic]]. Shakshouka is typically eaten with a [[Pita|pita]], like many other Middle Eastern dishes, or with white [[Bread|bread]] that is dipped in it.\n\n{{RecipeIngredients\n| 1 large [[Onion|onion]], peeled and finely chopped\n| [[Olive oil]]\n| 3 [[Cloves|cloves]] of [[Garlic]], peeled and crushed\n| 4 large [[Free range eggs|free range eggs]]\n| 1 small sweet [[Red pepper|red pepper]], deseeded and sliced finely\n| 6 medium [[Tomatoes|tomatoes]]\n| 1 tablespoon sweet [[Paprika powder|paprika powder]]\n| [[Sea Salt|sea salt]] and [[Freshly ground black pepper|freshly ground black pepper]] to taste\n}}\n===Method===\n\n{{RecipeMethod\n| Saut\u00e9 the [[Onions|onions]] and [[Garlic|garlic]] until soft in about a tablespoons of [[Olive oil|olive oil]], flavoured with a grind of [[Black pepper|black pepper]].  This will take about 8 minutes.\n| Meanwhile, grate the [[Tomatoes|tomatoes]] using the coarsest [[Grater|grater]] you have.\n| Add the [[Tomatoes|tomatoes]], [[Peppers|peppers]], [[Paprika|paprika]] and mix well.\n| Season to taste\n| Cover and [[Simmer|simmer]] on a very low heat for 25 minutes.\n| After 25 minutes, break the [[Eggs|eggs]] on to the surface of the [[Tomato|tomato]] mixture.\n| Cover and cook for 4 minutes or until the [[Eggs|eggs]] are done to your liking.\n}}\n===Variations===\nSome cooks like to break the [[Egg yolks|egg yolks]] and swirl them into the [[Tomatoes|tomatoes]].\n{{RecipeLine}}\n[[Category:Recipes]]\n[[Category:Egg recipes]]\n[[Category:Main courses]]\n[[Category:Middle Eastern recipes]]\n[[Category:North African recipes]]\n[[Category:Snacks and light bites]]\n[[Category:Starters]]\n[[Category:Tunisian recipes]]\n[[Category:Vegetable recipes]]\n[[Category:Vegetarian recipes]]\n[[Category:Pan fried]]\n\n\n<!-- footer hashtags --><code 'hashtagrev:032020'>#tomatoes #shakshouka #eggs #garlic #onions #oliveoil #peppers #pita #onion #eggyolks #paprikapowder </code><!-- /footer_hashtags -->"
