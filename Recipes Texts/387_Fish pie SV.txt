Preheat the water bath to 84\u00b0C (''183.2\u00b0F'')\n\n===Method===\n\n{{RecipeMethod\n\n| Put the [[Potatoes|potatoes]] in a vacuum packing pouch and smear [[Butter|butter]] over them.\n| Vacuum and seal.\n| Place in the water bath and cook leave for 1-2 hours or up to a maximum of 4.\n| Cool the water to 62\u00b0C (''143.6\u00b0F'') by removing some of the water and adding ice cubes or cold water, leaving the [[Potatoes|potatoes]] in the bath.\n| In the meantime, [[Vacuum pack|vacuum pack]] the [[Fish|fish]] in separate pouches and add to the water bath once it has reached 62\u00b0C (''143.6\u00b0F'').\n| After 30 minutes, remove the [[Prawns|prawns]], after a further 5-10 minutes remove the which [[Fish|fish]] and finally the [[Salmon|salmon]] after a further 10 minutes.\n| Also remove the [[Potatoes|potatoes]].\n| Place the pouches in s sink or bowls of iced water so that they can cool as soon as possible.\n| In the meantime, make the parsley sauce using the [[Parsley sauce|conventional method]] or with a [[Thermomix]].\n| Once the [[Fish|fish]] and [[Potatoes|potatoes]] are cool, remove them from the pouches.\n| Chop the [[Fish|fish]] into chunks and mix with the [[Prawns|prawns]].\n| Place in a [[Baking|baking]] dish and pour over the [[Parsley sauce|parsley sauce]].\n| [[Mash]] the [[Potatoes|potatoes]], adding extra [[Butter|butter]] and a little [[Milk|milk]] if desired.\n| Spread it over the [[Fish|fish]] mixture, dot with [[Butter|butter]] and [[Bake|bake]] at 200\u00b0C (''392\u00b0F'') for 30-40 minutes.\n}}\n===Serving
