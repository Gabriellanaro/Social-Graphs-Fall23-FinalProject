fillet]] [10 oz]\n| 1 [[leek]], halved along its length and cut into slices.\n| 2 cloves garlic, peeled and crushed.\n| 225g mashing [[potatoes]] such as Nadine, Saxon, Wilja, Desiree or King Edward, place]]===\n* Preheat the [[oven]] to  204\u00b0 C\t(400\u00b0 F - Gas 6)\n\n===Method===\n\n{{RecipeMethod\n| Cut the [[pork fillet]] into small slices\n| [[Fry]] the [[pork fillet]] slices in a little [[olive oil]] for 5 minutes\n| Add the leeks and [[Fry]] for another 4 minutes\n| Add the crushed garlic and stir fry for a minute of so\n| Add the mustard or your choice of spices to the flour and sprinkle over the pork and leeks.\n| Stir well to distribute the flour evenly\n| [[Deglaze]] the pan with the [[cider]]\n| Season with a pinch of salt and pepper.\n| Meanwhile, [[boil]] the [[potatoes]] and [[sweet potatoes]] in lightly [[salted]] water until they are soft and can be pierced easily with a fork\n| [[Mash]] the [[sweet potatoes]] and the [[potatoes]] together in the same bowl and add the [[butter]] and [[salt]] and [[pepper]] to taste\n| Layer half of the apple rings i=onto the base of an oven proof dish\n| Pour the pork mixture over the top\n| Layer the remaining apple on top of the pork mixture, top with [[mash]] [[potato]] and decorate with the tines of a fork\n| [[Bake]] in the oven for 1 hour minutes\n}}\n===Serving suggestion===\n\nServe hot with a green vegetable such as [[broccoli]] or [[peas]]..\n\n===Chef's notes===\n\nIf the [[leeks]] are large and a little 'tired', freshen them by [[blanching]] them in boiling water for 3 minutes-this stops them
