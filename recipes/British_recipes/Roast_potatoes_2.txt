minute OR put the potatoes in a [[Saucepan|saucepan]] and run the cold tap over them for 5 minutes.\n\n===Method===\n{{RecipeMethod\n\n| In a pan of fresh cold water, bring the [[Potatoes|potatoes]] to the [[Boil|boil]] and cook until they are fully cooked and just as they are about to fall apart.\n| Carefully transfer the [[Potatoes|potatoes]] (one by one) to a [[Colander|colander]].\n| Leave until any [[Steam|steam]] has disappeared.\n| Meanwhile, add plenty of [[dripping]] to a [[Roasting tin|roasting tin]] - you want a depth of about 5mm when melted, and sprinkle in some [[Salt|salt]].\n| Place the tin in the [[Oven|oven]] and preheat 250\u00b0 C (475\u00b0 F - gas 9), [fan oven 230\u00b0 C].\n| Once the [[Oven|oven]] has heated, quickly and gently transfer the [[Potatoes|potatoes]] to the pan and turn them over so that they are coated in [[dripping]].\n| Return the tin to the [[Oven|oven]] and reduce the oven temperature to 180\u00b0 C (350\u00b0 F - gas 4), [fan oven 160\u00b0 C].\n| [[Roast]] for about 1 hour - 1 hour 20 minutes, turning the [[Potatoes|potatoes]] every 20 minutes.  \n| The cooking times can vary, but the [[Potatoes|potatoes]] will be ready when they are crisp and golden all over.\n| Remove from the [[Oven|oven]] and serve.\n}}\n===Chef's notes===\nYou need to get to know your own [[Oven|oven]] as far as a cooking times are
