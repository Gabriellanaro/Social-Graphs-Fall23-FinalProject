Decide for yourself which one you would like to use as your standard [[Smoked|smoked]] [[Butter|butter]].\n\n{{RecipeIngredients\n\n| 250g [[Unsalted butter|unsalted butter]], divided into two 125g pieces, and roughly [[Diced|diced]]\n| 1 teaspoon [[Sherry vinegar|sherry vinegar]]\n| [[Apple]] or oak [[Smoking|smoking]] [[Chips|chips]], briefly soaked in water\n}}\n\n===Method===\n{{RecipeMethod\n\n| Place the [[Butter|butter]] in separate bowls.\n| Add the [[Sherry vinegar|sherry vinegar]] to one of the bowls.\n| Beat the [[Butter|butter]] which is in the bowl without the [[Vinegar|vinegar]], until soft.\n| Repeat the operation for the [[Butter|butter]] containing the [[Vinegar|vinegar]].\n| Cover both bowls with [[Cling film|cling film]] or an airtight lid.\n}}\n'''For each bowl'''\n{{RecipeMethod\n| Prepare the [[Smoking|smoking]] gun with the desired wood [[Chips|chips]].\n| Loosen up a little of the [[Cling film|cling film]], so that you have just enough room to insert the gun (you can use the tube if you like).\n| Light the wood [[Chips|chips]] with a lighter or match (do NOT use a blow torch or gas lighter).\n| Smoke for 15-20 seconds until the bowl is full of smoke.\n| Remove the gun and seal the gap with the [[Cling film|cling film]].\n| Leave for 10 minutes.\n| Repeat the previous three steps several times (up to 8) until you have the desired smokiness.\n| If not using immediately, remove the [[Butters|butters]] from the bowls, shape and wrap in [[Cling film|cling film]], or place in containers and keep [[Refrigerated|refrigerated]].\n}}\n===Serving suggestions===\nMelt on [[Meat|meat]] or [[Vegetables|vegetables]].  Also excellent with anything cooked on a [[barbecue]].\n===Variations===\nPlay about with different flavours of wood chips.
