Below is one of the simplest and therefore one of the most traditional.\n\nCooking [[Turkey|turkey]] and [[Stuffing|stuffing]] involves the risk of salmonella poisoning. To prevent this, cook the [[Turkey|turkey]] to 185 [[Degrees|degrees]] [[Fahrenheit]] and the [[Stuffing|stuffing]] to 165 [[Degrees|degrees]] [[Fahrenheit]]. Also, avoid cross contamination by keeping raw turkey away from all foods and wash your hands thoroughly after handling it.\n\nThe most effective way to measure food temperatures is with a digital [[Meat thermometer|meat thermometer]].\n\n{{RecipeIngredients\n\n| 8kg (18 pound) whole [[Turkey|turkey]]\n| &#189; cup [[Butter|butter]], softened\n| 1.5 quarts homemade [[Turkey|turkey]] [[Stock|stock]] or canned [[Chicken stock|chicken stock]]\n| [[Salt|salt]] and [[Freshly ground black pepper|freshly ground black pepper]] to taste\n}}\n===[[Mise en place]]===\n\n* Preheat the [[Oven|oven]] to 160\u00b0 C (325\u00b0 F - gas 3), [fan [[Oven|oven]] 140\u00b0 C & reduce cooking time by 10 mins per hour]\n\n\n===Method===\n{{RecipeMethod\n\n| Preheat [[Oven|oven]] to 325 [[Degrees|degrees]] [[Fahrenheit]]. Rub the skin with softened [[Butter|butter]], and season with [[Salt|salt]] and [[Pepper|pepper]].\n| Fill the body cavity with [[Stuffing|stuffing]].\n| Place a [[Meat thermometer|meat thermometer]] in the thickest part of one [[Thigh|thigh]].\n| Position an [[Aluminium foil| aluminium foil]] tent over the [[Turkey|turkey]].\n| Place the [[Turkey|turkey]] in the [[Oven|oven]], and pour two cups of [[Stock|stock]] into the bottom of the [[Roasting pan|roasting pan]].\n| [[Baste]] the entire [[Turkey|turkey]] every 30 minutes with the juices on the bottom of the pan.\n| Add [[Stock|stock]] to the drippings as they become dry, about one to two cups at a time.\n| Remove [[Aluminium foil| aluminium foil]] tent after two and a half hours.\n| [[Roast]] until the [[Meat|meat]] reaches 185 [[Degrees|degrees]] [[Fahrenheit]] and the [[Stuffing|stuffing]] reaches 165 [[Degrees|degrees]] [[Fahrenheit]].
