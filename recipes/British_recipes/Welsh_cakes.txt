[[Griddle|griddle]]-cakes are also known as \"bakestones\" as they were originally cooked on a hot stone or cat-iron [[griddle]].\n\n{{RecipeIngredients\n| 8oz (225 g) [[Self-raising [[Caster sugar|caster sugar]]\n| [[Butter|butter]] for greasing\n}}\n===Method===\n\n{{RecipeMethod\n| Rub the [[Fat|fat]] into the sieved [[Flour|flour]] to until it resembles [[Breadcrumbs|breadcrumbs]].\n| Add to a bowl with the [[Sugar|sugar]], [[Dried fruit|dried fruit]] and mix well.\n| Add the beaten [[Egg|egg]]\n| Combine using your hands and form a ball of [[Dough|dough]].\n| If you need to loosen the [[Dough|dough]] a little, use a splash of [[Milk|milk]].\n| Roll out the [[Pastry|pastry]] to a &frac14;\" (5 mm) thick and cut into rounds with a 3-4i\" (7.5-10 cm) pastry cutter.\n| Grease a heavy [[Griddle|griddle]] or [[Tava|tava]] with with [[Butter|butter]]\n| Place on direct heat until it is quite hot\n| Cook the Welsh cakes on the [[Griddle|griddle]], turning once, about 2 to 3 minutes on each side.\n| Each side needs to be [[Browned|browned]] before it's turned over.\n| Remove and dust with [[Caster sugar|caster sugar]] while still warm.\n}}\n===Serving suggestions===\nSplit in half and serve while still warm with [[Butter|butter]] and [[Jam|jam]].\n{{RecipeLine}}\n[[Category:Recipes]]\n[[Category:Bread and
