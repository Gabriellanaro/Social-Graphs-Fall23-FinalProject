Broadly translated, it refers to food which has been pounded. The definition of the word 'Pachadi' is different among different South Indian regions. While in Kerala and Tamilnadu, pachadi is a side-dish [[Curry|curry]] similar to the north Indian [[Raita]], in Andhra Pradesh, pachadi is a fresh [[Pickle|pickle]] and has very low shelf life, a maximum of two or three days. It is made of fresh [[Vegetables|vegetables]] and is served as an [[Accompaniment|accompaniment]] for [[Rice|rice]], [[Snacks|snacks]] like idli, [[Dosa|dosa]], pesarattu.\n\nIn Kerala and Tamilnadu states pachadi is eaten fresh and typically made of finely chopped and [[Boiled|boiled]] [[Vegetables|vegetables]] with [[Coconut|coconut]], green or [[Red chillies|red chillies]] and tempered in [[Oil|oil]] with [[Mustard seeds|mustard seeds]], [[Ginger|ginger]] and [[Curry leaves|curry leaves]]. [[Curd]]/[[Yogurt|yogurt]] based pachadi can be made of any [[Vegetable|vegetable]], although [[Cucumbers|cucumbers]], [[Squash|squash]], [[Mango|mango]], [[Bitter|bitter]] gourd or [[Pineapple|pineapple]] are common.
