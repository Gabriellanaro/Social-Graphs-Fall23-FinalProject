There is a widespread belief among Hispanics in the US that it originated in Puerto Rico but there is no direct evidence for this. However, it resembles [[Paella|paella]], a [[Spanish]] dish, both in its ingredients and cooking technique, and may very well be a New World adaptation of it.\n\nCommon ingredients for arroz con pollo include [[Rice|rice]], [[Vegetables|vegetables]], [[Fresh herbs|fresh herbs]] and [[Chicken|chicken]].\n\n{{RecipeIngredients\n| 1.5 kg whole [[Chicken|chicken]] cut into serving pieces - [[Jointing a chicken|see here for step by step details]]\n| [[Salt|salt]] and [[Pepper|pepper]] to taste\n| [[Olive oil|olive oil]]\n| 4 [[Cloves|cloves]] of fresh, minced [[Garlic|garlic]]\n| 1 medium [[Onion|onion]], chopped\n| 8 oz [[Diced|diced]] [[Red bell pepper|red bell pepper]]\n| 1 can (15 ounce) [[Diced|diced]] [[Tomatoes|tomatoes]]\n| &#189; tablespoon sweet [[Paprika|paprika]]\n| &#189; oz chopped [[Cilantro|cilantro]]\n| 6 cups [[Chicken|chicken]] [[Bouillon|bouillon]]\n| a handful of [[Cilantro|cilantro]]\n| 8 [[Saffron|saffron]] threads or 1 teaspoon of food colouring for [[Yellow rice|yellow rice]]\n| 2 cups medium or long-rain [[Rice|rice]]\n| 8 oz canned [[Peas|peas]] (thoroughly drained)\n}}\n===Method===\n\n{{RecipeMethod\n| Season the [[Chicken|chicken]] with two pinches of [[Salt|salt]] and a pinch of [[Pepper|pepper]].\n| Pour enough [[Olive oil|olive oil]] into a large [[Skillet|skillet]] to just barely cover the bottom.\n| Saut\u00e9 [[Chicken|chicken]] in [[Oil|oil]] until [[Brown|brown]]. Bring to a rolling [[Boil|boil]].\n| Add the [[Rice|rice]] and mix well. [[Simmer]] over medium heat until the rice is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.
