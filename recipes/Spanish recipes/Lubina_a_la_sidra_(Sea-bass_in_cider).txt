Since the 17th century [[cider]] has been commercially produced in north-west Spain (Galicia, Asturias and the Basque country) and they even have special bars called sider\u00edas.  This recipe is a simple version of a dish from the Asturias.\n\n{{RecipeIngredients\n| 4 [[Sea bass|sea bass]] fillets\n| [[Flour]] \n| [[Salt]] and [[Pepper|pepper]]\n| 2 tablespoons [[Butter|butter]] \n| 2 [[Onions|onions]] finely [[Diced|diced]]\n| [[Garlic]], to taste, finely diced (I used 2 bulbs)\n| A few [[saffron]] strands, crushed\n| 500 ml dry [[Cider|cider]]\n}}\n===Method===\n\n{{RecipeMethod\n| Clean and dry the sea-[[Bass|bass]] fillets and lightly coat them with seasoned [[Flour|flour]].\n| Melt the [[Butter|butter]] in a pan and seal the [[Fish|fish]] until they are golden.\n| Remove from the pan, place in greased [[tin foil]] and put in a [[Baking|baking]] dish. \n| Add the [[Onions|onions]] and [[Garlic|garlic]] to the pan and [[Fry|fry]] until soft, then add the [[saffron]] and half of the [[Cider|cider]].\n| Allow to reduce and then pour it over the [[Fish|fish]], together with the remaining [[Cider|cider]].\n| Wrap the [[tin foil]] loosely and [[Bake|bake]] in the oven for 15 minutes at 180 C/325 F/Gas 4\n}}\n===Variations===\nIt is also traditional to place some peeled and sliced reineta [[Apple|apple]] (use Cox's [[Orange|orange]] pippin in the UK) on top of the [[Fish|fish]] when [[Baking|baking]].  You can also add shellfish and [[Paprika|paprika]].\n===Chef's notes===\nUse Asturian [[cider]] if possible.
